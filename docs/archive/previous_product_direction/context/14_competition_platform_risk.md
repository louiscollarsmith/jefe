# 14 — Competition and Platform Risk

## Shopify Sidekick / Pulse / Campaign Autopilot

Threat:
- free
- native
- broad distribution
- platform data
- very fast shipping velocity

Avoid collision:
- be the accountable operator
- House Rules
- holdouts
- cross-tool actions
- verified P&L
- agency workflows
- non-Shopify channels

Assumption:
- native AI may take 18–24 months to become credible at cross-tool execution
- be defensible by then

## Triple Whale / Polar

Threat:
- strong analytics
- AI operating-system positioning

Avoid collision:
- sell conclusions and verified actions to founder-operators
- do not sell another analytics suite

## Klaviyo AI

Threat:
- strong lifecycle execution
- attribution claims
- first write path runs through Klaviyo

Avoid collision:
- use Klaviyo as write path #1
- measure it independently
- keep fallback write path on risk register, even if weak
- platform risk here is permissioned, not just competitive

## Agencies

Threat:
- can manually perform many actions

Avoid collision:
- sequence agency approach carefully
- white-label proof tooling first
- agency console later

## Ways this dies

- daily-brief habit never forms
- brief opens under 40%
- verification is underpowered and product feels like claims tool
- service expectation drowns the company
- Shopify/Klaviyo squeeze API permissions
- one uncapped customer-facing mistake kills trust
- froth window closes before proof-first raise
